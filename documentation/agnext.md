# What is AgNext?

2/4 Judges are from AgNext, outside of the comp sci department.

https://agnext.colostate.edu/

## TL;DR

AgNext is a research collaborative at Colorado State University (founded 2020) focused on **sustainable animal agriculture**. They partner with producers, industry, and policymakers to scale science-based innovations for animal/ecosystem health, economic viability, and resilient food systems.

- **Director:** Dr. Kim Stackhouse-Lawson (formerly JBS USA Director of Sustainability; NCBA Exec Director of Global Sustainability)
- **Departments:** Animal Sciences, College of Agricultural Sciences, College of Veterinary Medicine & Biomedical Sciences (NOT computer science)

## Mission

Identify and scale innovation that fosters the health of animals and ecosystems to promote profitable industries that support vibrant communities.

## Focus Areas

1. **Stewardship & Resilience** — animal, human, and ecosystem health; food security
2. **Data-Driven Solutions** — measure, verify, validate outcomes for transparency
3. **Communication & Stakeholder Engagement** — storytelling, publications, collaboration

## Research Areas

- Agricultural Economics
- Dairy Systems
- Feedlot Systems
- Grazing Systems & Rangeland Management
- Greenhouse Gas Measurement & Mitigation (esp. **enteric methane**)
- Modeling & Life Cycle Assessment

## Key Facility

**Climate-Smart Research Facility at ARDEC** — largest climate-smart research facility in the U.S., built with >$1M of donated industry equipment. Specialized for measuring enteric methane emissions in both feedlot and grazing systems.

## Existing Tools They've Built

- Feed Additive Calculator Tool (FACT) for Beef
- Feed Additive Calculator Tool (FACT) for Dairy
- Price Slide Tool for Marketing Steers
- Grazing Principles resources

## What This Means for Judging

Two of four judges will evaluate from a **livestock sustainability / ag science** lens, not a CS lens. They will likely care about:

- Real-world applicability to ranchers, feedlots, dairies, or rangeland managers
- Measurable environmental outcomes (methane, carbon, water, land use)
- Data quality, verification, and transparency (their "data-driven solutions" pillar)
- Producer economics and profitability — solutions that pencil out
- Clear communication of impact (avoid jargon-heavy CS pitches)
- Bonus: anything touching net zero, carbon markets, virtual fence, heat stress, or LCA
