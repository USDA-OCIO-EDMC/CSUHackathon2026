# FruitGuard Feature Report

FruitGuard is a geospatial risk intelligence prototype for USDA PPQ fruit fly surveillance. It combines ArcGIS feature layers, hackathon-provided movement and pest datasets, a transparent port risk index, temperature suitability rules, predictive pathway modeling, and the Fruit & Fly response concept into two browser applications:

- `fruitguard_live.html`: operational port-risk dashboard for current inspection and response prioritization.
- `ml_dashboard.html`: predictive country-origin to U.S.-port pathway dashboard for remaining-2026 risk forecasting.

This report documents every major feature implemented in those two applications, how each feature works, what data it uses, what math or modeling supports it, and how the work addresses the judging criteria.

## Executive Summary

FruitGuard answers one operational question:

> Which foreign-origin fruit fly pathways and U.S. ports should PPQ prioritize for inspection, early detection, and Fruit & Fly decoy-layer response?

The solution is built around four linked ideas:

1. Fruit fly risk is a pathway problem. Risk enters through origin countries, passenger flow, cargo or fruit imports, and U.S. ports of entry.
2. Fruit fly biology matters. Below 40F, the app treats outdoor destination establishment risk as filtered out. The 40F to 45F band is marginal, and 45F+ is actionable for response planning.
3. Geospatial context matters. The app displays U.S. ports, origin-to-port pathways, ArcGIS layers, port coordinates, source country pressure, and temperature-suitability filters.
4. Outputs must be actionable. The dashboards do not stop at scores. They translate risk into port queues, pathway rankings, response briefs, hot spot lists, Fruit & Fly staging decisions, and evidence panels.

Current generated outputs from the latest run:

- `fruitguard_live.html` uses 8 U.S. port records and 3 configured ArcGIS source layer groups.
- `ml_dashboard.html` uses 960 forecast pathway-month rows for May through December 2026.
- 27 forecast pathway-months are ranked HIGH or CRITICAL.
- 90 pathway-months are below 40F and are shown only when the "Below 40F filtered out" temperature class is selected.
- The default ML view excludes below-40F rows and shows 870 actionable pathway-months.

## Domain Understanding and Biology

### Fruit fly biology represented in the app

Fruit flies are not treated as generic pests. The dashboards encode several biological realities:

- Survival and establishment depend on temperature.
- Host fruit availability and movement volume matter.
- Species matter because different fruit fly groups have different lure and pathway implications.
- Peak warm-season months matter for surveillance and response timing.
- Foreign source pressure matters because invasive fruit fly risk begins before arrival at the U.S. port.

### Temperature rule

The application implements a domain rule supplied during project development:

- Below 40F: filtered out. Fruit fly survival/establishment risk is treated as zero for pathway ranking.
- 40F to 45F: marginal suitability. The model keeps the pathway but downweights it.
- 45F and above: suitable/actionable for pathway response planning.

In `fruitguard_ml.py`, the dashboard-facing temperature reclassification is:

```text
if temp_f < 40:
    suitability = 0.0
elif temp_f < 45:
    suitability = 0.35
else:
    suitability = 1.0
```

The below-40F rows are assigned:

```text
risk_tier = FILTERED_OUT
risk_label = "Below 40F filtered out"
temperature_adjusted_detections = 0
pathway_priority_score = 0
recommended_action = "No Fruit & Fly deployment; below 40F temperature mask, retain inbound inspection only"
```

Important UI behavior:

- In `ml_dashboard.html`, "All actionable temperature classes" excludes below-40F rows by default.
- Below-40F rows appear only when the user explicitly selects the "Below 40F filtered out" temperature class.
- Those rows render in grey instead of green so they are not mistaken for low risk.

### Species and lure context

The live dashboard maps dominant species groups to lure context:

- `Bactrocera dorsalis` and related species -> methyl eugenol.
- `Ceratitis capitata` and `Anastrepha` groups -> trimedlure.
- `Bactrocera cucurbitae` -> cuelure.
- Unknown/mixed species -> methyl eugenol / trimedlure.

The UI presents this as response context for Fruit & Fly decoy apples. It does not specify a chemical formulation or operational recipe; it communicates the concept at a safe, high-level planning level.

## Fruit & Fly Response Concept

Fruit & Fly is the value-added response concept integrated into both dashboards.

Concept summary:

- Pheromone-baited decoy apples hang above a protected catch layer inside a cargo box.
- The layer beneath the decoys prevents captured flies or fallen decoys from mixing with marketable fruit.
- Side emitters provide male wing-frequency cues and acoustic activity estimates.
- When the system estimates that most flies are clustered near the decoys, the containment layer closes.
- The response goal is to isolate flies away from good fruit and create a practical port/cargo intervention layer.

Where it appears:

- Live dashboard header: `FruitGuard + Fruit & Fly`.
- Live sidebar: `Fruit & Fly Decoy Layer`.
- Live deployment tab: cargo-box decoy apple layer explanation.
- Live port cards: `DEPLOY FRUIT & FLY LAYER`, `MONITOR - STAGE DECOY LAYER`, or `STANDBY - INSPECTION ONLY`.
- ML dashboard response brief: pathway-specific response recommendation.
- ML hot spots: ports prioritized for Fruit & Fly staging.

Decision logic:

For the live dashboard:

```text
if CPRI >= 70:
    decoyStatus = "deploy"
elif CPRI >= 50:
    decoyStatus = "monitor"
else:
    decoyStatus = "standby"
```

For the ML dashboard:

```text
CRITICAL -> Deploy Fruit & Fly decoy layer immediately and intensify inspection
HIGH -> Stage Fruit & Fly decoy layer and increase targeted surveillance
MEDIUM -> Monitor with focused inspection; keep decoy layers ready
LOW -> Routine inspection; no decoy layer deployment
FILTERED_OUT -> No Fruit & Fly deployment; below 40F temperature mask
```

### Feature 3: Composite Pathway Risk Index (CPRI)

Implemented in:

- `compute_cpri()` in `run_pipeline.py`

What it does:

- Converts multiple risk indicators into a 0-100 port-level score.
- Creates a transparent operational index that can be explained in a judging demo.

Formula:

```text
det_score    = min((detection_count / 50) * 100, 100)
route_score  = min((route_count / 120) * 100, 100)
region_score = origin_region_risk
pass_score   = min((passenger_volume / 1,000,000) * 100, 100)
cargo_score  = min((cargo_volume / 500,000) * 100, 100)

CPRI =
    0.35 * det_score
  + 0.25 * route_score
  + 0.25 * region_score
  + 0.10 * pass_score
  + 0.05 * cargo_score
```

Risk tiers:

```text
CPRI >= 85 -> CRITICAL
CPRI >= 70 -> HIGH
CPRI >= 50 -> MEDIUM
else       -> LOW
```

Why these indicators:

- Detection history captures observed pest pressure.
- Route volume captures entry opportunity.
- Origin region risk captures foreign source pressure.
- Passenger volume captures high-frequency movement pathways.
- Cargo/fruit import volume captures host-material movement pressure.

Judging value:

- Evidence-based risk framework.
- Transparent weights.
- Operationally interpretable scores.
- Easy to defend and adjust if PPQ subject-matter experts prefer different weights.

### Feature 2: Weak-supervision training panel

Implemented in:

- `complete_pathway_panel()` in `fruitguard_ml.py`

Problem solved:

- The provided detections are port/month totals, not confirmed origin-country detections.
- The model still needs to estimate which origin-country pathways are most responsible for port risk.

Approach:

- Build a complete panel of:
  - origin_country
  - us_port
  - month_start
- Join passenger volume, fruit imports, pest status, and port detections.
- Attribute port detections across origin pathways using pressure features.

Attribution math:

```text
passenger_norm = passengers / max(passengers in month)
trade_norm     = fruit_imports / max(fruit_imports in month)

pathway_pressure =
    0.55 * passenger_norm
  + 0.30 * trade_norm
  + 0.15 * pest_score

weighted_pressure = pathway_pressure * (0.30 + pest_score)

attribution_share =
    weighted_pressure / sum(weighted_pressure for same port-month)

attributed_detections =
    port_detections * attribution_share
```

Why this is rigorous:

- It does not pretend the data has labels it does not have.
- It uses a documented weak-supervision strategy.
- It explicitly states the limitation in `model_report.txt`.
- It keeps the attribution method visible in the generated data.

Judging value:

- Demonstrates analytical honesty.
- Uses provided sources despite imperfect labels.
- Produces useful pathway rankings while documenting uncertainty.

## Limitations and Honest Caveats

FruitGuard is a hackathon prototype, not an official PPQ operational system. The current implementation is intentionally transparent about limitations:

- Port detections do not identify the exact origin country of each individual interception.
- Country-origin pathway labels are inferred by weak supervision from passenger, cargo, and pest pressure.
- Temperature suitability currently uses monthly port-temperature proxies, not full gridded raster weather layers.
- The training dataset is small and imbalanced.
- Some features, such as inspection effort, trap density, commodity details, shipment contents, and port staffing, are not available but would improve model performance.
- The Fruit & Fly concept is represented as a response-planning idea, not a detailed biological or chemical protocol.

Why this is still valuable:

- It gives PPQ analysts a transparent framework they can critique and improve.
- It demonstrates how live geospatial data, movement data, pest status, climate suitability, and ML can be combined.
- It produces concrete, ranked, actionable outputs even with imperfect hackathon data.

## Scalability Roadmap

FruitGuard can scale in several directions:

1. Add gridded climate rasters.
   - Replace monthly airport temperature proxies with raster-derived minimum temperature layers.
   - Use the existing array reclassification functions for cell-by-cell suitability.

2. Add historical APHIS/PPQ interception records.
   - Improve labels and reduce weak-supervision uncertainty.
   - Validate pathway risk across multiple years.

3. Add inspection effort and trap density.
   - Distinguish true pest pressure from higher detection caused by higher inspection intensity.

4. Add commodity-specific cargo data.
   - Weight pathways by host fruit availability and shipment type.

5. Add live endpoint scoring.
   - Use SageMaker endpoint mode for real-time prediction from new route/port records.

6. Add scenario planning.
   - Simulate what happens if a new origin country becomes "Present" or if a port enters a warmer month.

7. Add official response workflow fields.
   - Let PPQ experts attach operating procedures, inspection notes, or response status to each port/pathway.

8. Add model monitoring.
   - Compare forecasted risk with future detections.
   - Track drift in route pressure, temperature suitability, and pest status.
